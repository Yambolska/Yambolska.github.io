Mobile Game User Behavior, Monetization & Error Analysis

Case Study · Mid-Core Mobile FPS · 2.45M+ Users

Overview

This case study analyzes player behavior in a mid-core mobile FPS game to understand engagement, retention, monetization patterns and the impact of technical stability on user experience. Using a 29-day event window, more than 7.29M daily activity rows were aggregated into a user-level dataset and connected to business outcomes such as ARPU, ARPPU and revenue concentration.

Methodology

The analysis combines behavioral feature engineering, clustering, funnel analysis and error-bucket profiling:

  • Built user-level metrics: days_played, sessions_per_day, avg_session_duration, play_intensity, match_intensity, win_rate, completion_rate, error_rate and total_revenue (IAP + ads)
  • Performed user segmentation (K-Means) to distinguish trial users, F2P core, heavy spenders and VIP / whale clusters
  • Evaluated monetization via payer rate, ARPU, ARPPU, whale share and revenue share by cluster
  • Ran Install → Session → Match → Payer funnels and Time-to-First-Purchase (TTFP) by segment
  • Compared country and platform (iOS vs Android) on engagement, ARPU, payer rate and error_rate
  • Linked server_connection_error buckets to retention and revenue to quantify the impact of technical issues

Key Insights

  • The game follows a classic F2P pattern: many low-engagement trial users, with revenue concentrated in a small heavy / whale core (≈1.4% payer rate, ARPPU 30+).
  • Behavioral metrics clearly separate “try-and-leave” users from long-term adopters; whales show high play_intensity, win_rate and sessions_per_day.
  • TTFP is typically 30–60 days, and VIPs convert even later, confirming a retention-first, monetization-second model.
  • Error analysis shows that mid_error users are heavy, valuable players, while extreme_error users churn quickly with near-zero revenue — severe instability is lethal for both retention and monetization.
  • Country and platform breakdowns highlight high-value markets (e.g. US, Türkiye, Brazil) and regions where high error_rate blocks monetization, especially on Android.

These findings translate into concrete product recommendations for onboarding and funnel optimization, live-ops and offer design for whales, regional pricing and localization, and technical stability priorities — linking game design and infrastructure decisions directly to user retention and revenue growth.
